Simple But Overlooked Communication Practices

How I wish my colleagues communicated

Home Page

I often think that communication is a skill that requires complex and arcane practices. However, I see time and time again that it is more about the simplest imaginable practices that are so common sense, yet completely overlooked.

First, I see that I and my colleagues often forget to introduce ourselves during meetings. Just a few words: first name, who you are, and why you are here. This already helps clear so much confusion. I often find myself in a situation where I am completely unaware who the people I am talking to are and why.

Second, after a meeting it is important to summarize and provide actionable items. This makes sure the meeting was not just about talking, but about defining steps.

Third, simplify. All information can be simplified. Yes, sometimes you have to use complex language to demonstrate your competence. But it doesn't hurt to then simplify immediately after.

Fourth, be transparent. I try to add as many relevant notes from communication to JIRA tickets or say them out loud. There are so many chats and various channels. Information simply gets lost there.

So here is my simple wish. Introduce yourself. Summarize action items. Simplify what you just said. And be transparent about where information lives. These are not advanced techniques. They are just the basics that are extremely effective.
