Learn Like Humans not Machines

Notes on what is important in education

Home Page

Learning should come from a personal, very human motivation. Not an abstract one.

Memorizing books, words, or mathematics for the sake of a job or competition is completely besides the point. In fact, it will make you hate learning.

Learning should be an interesting adventure.

Take learning Chinese as an example. Some people brag about how quickly they learned it to a high level of mastery. This makes those who cannot catch up feel quite inadequate. But learning a language is particularly hard. It is a confluence of so many factors: your previous background, your ontology, your associations, your circle of friends, your resources. Even your own mind can reject a language for various reasons.

So instead of forcing your children to pick up skills, try a different approach. Show them that they can achieve success in learning something they truly want. Help them set ambitious but smart goals and then reach them. This way, they will not think of learning as talent versus non-talent. They will see it as something they can plan for themselves. They will feel confident they can succeed with effort and dedication. And they will not even need external validation. That is the ideal scenario, of course.

It is important to remember we are not robots. We are people.

The fascination on the internet — watching people perform robotic tasks masterfully — only makes this problem worse. We need to shift our fascination. Not toward robotic things like memorizing thousands of words instantly or playing guitar extremely fast. Instead, we should be fascinated by human features: efficient communication in a language, simplicity, meaning, humility, wisdom, and kindness in all these acts.

That is the real mastery. And it starts with remembering why we learn in the first place.
