Imagine a street. Not just any street, but a Street of a Thousand Tables. You are hungry. Your stomach gives a polite, but insistent, cough. Lunchtime has arrived.

You stand at the beginning of this street. To your left: bread. To your right: soup. Straight ahead: a hundred colors of ice cream, twenty kinds of dumplings, and a man selling roasted chestnuts from a cart that looks like a small steam engine.

Now, here is the strange thing. You are free. You can have anything. Yet you do not move. You are not happy. You are, in fact, beginning to suffer.

Why? Is it laziness? No. It is a much more interesting disease. Let us call it the _Roulette of the Open Menu_.

**The Engine of Choice**

In your chest, beneath the buttons of your coat, there is a small engine. It is not your heart. It is your Will. And like any engine—like the little motor in a fan or the piston in a locomotive—it runs on fuel. The fuel is _effort_. When you are hungry, you have one goal: to stop being hungry. That is a simple fire. You need only a small spark of will: _walk to the bread, break the bread, eat._

But the Street of a Thousand Tables tricks you. It whispers, "Yes, but what if the _second_ bowl of soup is better than the first? What if the twelfth dumpling is the real treasure?"

Now your poor engine is not driving you forward. It is spinning its wheels. It is overheating. You are no longer choosing food. You are gambling. And gambling is the enemy of will.

**The Two Rules of the Wise Mechanic**

A wise person (let us imagine an old engineer with oil on his fingers and a calm face) knows how to tend this engine. He does not fight the street. He simply follows two laws.

_First Law: Know Your Boiler._
What is the goal? To satisfy hunger. That is all. The goal is not "to have a mystical experience with a chestnut." The goal is not "to win the argument of lunch." The goal is fuel. As soon as a dish meets your simple criteria (it is food, it is ready, it is not poison), the search is over. The engine can rest.

_Second Law: The Real is Better than the Ghost._
The mind loves a ghost. The ghost is the Perfect Option that hides behind the next door. It is never found. It is a trick of the imagination. The real bread in your hand—warm, solid, real—is worth more than a thousand ghost dumplings. A choice made is a locomotive moving forward. A choice postponed is a locomotive rusting on the tracks.

**The Greatest Wisdom**

They say that in old libraries, the wisest books are not the thickest. They are the ones that can tell you what to _ignore_.

Life is the Street of a Thousand Tables. Every day, it offers you freedom. And freedom, my friend, is a heavy machine. It is not the absence of rules. It is the presence of a goal.

When you learn to sort the important from the non-important, the world becomes quiet. The thousand voices stop shouting. You see the one loaf of bread. You break it. You eat.

And that small, simple act—that is not a compromise. That is victory over the roulette. That is will, working like a perfect, silent piston.

Choose wisely. Which means: choose simply. Then go eat your lunch. It is already warm, and it is waiting for you.
