I have a strong dislike for how most people invest their money. Stocks and cryptocurrency are popular choices, but I see them as a huge waste of time. They offer no real guarantees. It is no secret that the market is often supported by inexperienced people who make mistakes and lose money. They are encouraged to join, and their losses become someone else's gain. I think about the YouTube channel "Low Budget Stories" and the character Wojak. He is always trying to escape being a wage slave, but he keeps losing in that game. There is a lot of truth in that picture.

Because of this, I think about investments differently. I look for ways that are low risk but have a high reward. I have built my own investment pyramid to reflect this.

First, invest in yourself. Gain knowledge, learn new skills, and collect experiences you can use anywhere. This is the foundation. Next, make sure you have a place to live. A home and a small piece of land to grow food is the ultimate safety net. It means you will never end up living under a bridge. Finally, invest in people. Strong relationships with family and friends can help you overcome almost any problem. These are the most important investments I can think of.

But I have been thinking about another option. It is investing in collectibles. This means keeping things that hold memories, but that might also grow in value over a long time. They can simply serve as decorations in your home. The key is to not get too attached to them. You just need to remember that your goal is to preserve value, not to hold onto them forever.

Think about it this way. If I had collected Pokemon cards as a kid, I would have a great collection now. If I had collected those old wagon wheel cowboy figures, I would have a valuable set. The kids who loved those things have now grown up. Many of them have good jobs and extra money to spend. They might be willing to pay good money to own the things they loved as children, things that are hard to find in stores anymore.

This approach combines a fun hobby with a real way to protect your money. It is not as easily manipulated by the big players who control the stock market. It feels more real and more personal.

So the next step for me is simple. I need to figure out where to start. I need to learn how to do this the right way. I want to make sure I am collecting things that will hold value, not just gathering trash.
