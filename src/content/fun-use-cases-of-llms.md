LLMs are an extraordinary tool. It is hard to believe that less than 5 years ago we didn't have them and now it is hard to imagine our lives without them.

An extraordinary intelligence at your finger tips that can output text. Seemingly boring, but in reality absolutely limitless.

One way I have used LLMs in creative way so far is like this:

**Visualization apps**

It has always been hard for me to work with Chinese family tree, so I create an app that visualizes relationships where your position in the tree can change. The LLM helps me build it fast, and I can finally see the connections clearly.

Another example, I want to do parallel reading in Chinese and English, but there aren't useful apps out there - so why not create it from scratch. Done. Now I have exactly what I need, no extra features, no clutter.

I created an editor and visualizer for a decision tree where I can create decision trees for my daily routines easily. It helps me think through my mornings step by step.

I also created a decision tree for buying a car or similar. And it is pretty awesome. I just answer a few questions and it points me in the right direction.

**Software**

I don't like how React promised to be just a view layer and turned into a convoluted framework, so why not create your own view layer Reactive component implementation yourself that is well tested and just fits your need + seem that can allow to replace if needed. In fact, instead of relying on libraries that tailor to everyone, it seems a much better idea to rely on your own implementation that is well tested and simple subset of the crazy amount of features libraries cover. It is simple, easy and is done under 5 minutes. The LLM handles the boilerplate, and I get exactly what I want.

Writing IDE plugins has never been easier, I created a plugin that allows to view file structure based on declarative comments in files, similar to how tag section works in my blog. It is proves that FSD is a wrong ideology (wrote an article about it). The LLM wrote most of the plugin logic while I focused on the design.

**Personal**

I provide LLM with my goals and my understanding direction and ask him to help me create action items to tackle these goals. It breaks down big ambitions into small steps I can start on today.

**Boring Documents**

I ask LLMs to rewrite boring documents as soviet union style textbooks with smooth narrative flow.

**Other**

Other people have come up with a myriad of creative ways to use LLMs. For example: generating custom bedtime stories for their kids, building personal chatbots that speak in their own voice, or creating tools to translate old family letters they never understood before. The possibilities really are endless.
